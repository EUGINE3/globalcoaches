Leadership Foundations

Integrity & Character

"Leadership is not about being in charge. It is about taking care of those in your charge."
- Simon Sinek

Learning Content

This week introduces the foundational concepts of leadership, focusing on character development and integrity as the cornerstone of effective leadership. Students will explore different leadership styles and their applications in various contexts.

Key Topics:
  • Defining transformational leadership
  • Character vs. competence in leadership
  • Leadership styles and situational application
  • Building trust and credibility
Resources
  • Video: "The Power of Character in Leadership" (15 mins)
  • Reading: "Leadership Foundations" (Chapter 1)
  • Podcast: "Building Authentic Leadership" (25 mins)
Weekly Assignment
100 pts

Personal Leadership Assessment: Complete a comprehensive self-assessment of your current leadership style and identify three areas for development. Submit a 500-word reflection paper.

Vision & Purpose

Clarity & Direction

"Where there is no vision, the people perish."
- Proverbs 29:18

Learning Content

Explore the critical role of vision in leadership and learn how to develop, communicate, and implement a compelling vision that inspires action and drives change.

Key Topics:
  • Crafting a compelling vision statement
  • Aligning purpose with action
  • Communicating vision effectively
  • Overcoming resistance to change
Weekly Assignment
100 pts

Vision Development Exercise: Create a personal or organizational vision statement and develop a strategic plan for implementation. Present your vision in a 3-minute video presentation.
